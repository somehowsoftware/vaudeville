# Davar Chadash

In rabbinic law, a *davar chadash* — "a new thing" — is a case existing precedent was never built to reach. The term names a jurisprudential emergency: faced with a genuinely novel case, legal reasoning stretches the nearest analogy until it appears to cover, and the stretched analogy delivers confident wrong answers. The category exists to license the harder move — stop stretching, reason from first principles. Vaudeville keeps the term because the situation is the same. The framework's generative axiom, stated compressed in [the doctrine](../doctrine/bearing/davar-chadash.md) and worked practice by practice through [the map](../map.md), is that the AI agent is a davar chadash: software-engineering practice is calibrated to humans writing code, management practice to persistent living beings, and neither reference class reaches the agent. Every engineering practice therefore deserves re-derivation from the agent's cost structure rather than inheritance — landing sometimes on the familiar answer, sometimes on one the industry abandoned, sometimes on one with no precedent. This essay is the argument.

## The mechanism is entanglement

A human practice is a theorem proved under assumptions nobody wrote down, because they never varied: fluency travels with self-knowledge, competence is roughly smooth, more memory is better, wording is style. Each bundles facts that were only ever coupled in humans. The axiom claims the agent decouples them: what reads as one incontrovertible truth is often two. The claim is empirical; the evidence comes with contrary findings, weighed as the argument goes, because they do work of their own.

**Capability decouples from itself.** In a preregistered field experiment with 758 consultants (Dell'Acqua et al., Organization Science, 2026), AI assistance raised output quality 32 percent on tasks inside the model's competence while cutting correctness by 14 to 25 percent on an adjacent task that looked no harder — the "jagged technology frontier" is that paper's coinage. In humans, last week's performance predicts this week's — the inference behind every reputation system — and here it fails. The practice it changes: trust is granted task-locally, never to the worker in general, so the framework frames every unit of work explicitly by kind and discretion ([Assignments](../doctrine/vocabulary.md#assignment)) rather than handing an undifferentiated ticket to a trusted generalist.

**The window is not a working memory.** The context window — everything an agent can see while it works — invites the inherited intuition that more retained material is more asset. Raw accumulation degrades: models use long contexts position-sensitively, worst in the middle (Liu et al., TACL, 2024); of 17 models tested on the RULER benchmark (COLM, 2024), only half stayed satisfactory at 32,000 tokens despite claiming windows at least that large; and when a benchmark removes surface word-overlap so retrieval must be semantic (NoLiMa, a 2025 preprint), 11 of 13 models fell below half their own short-context baseline. The other side is just as real: curated context keeps paying — many-shot in-context learning improves through thousands of examples (Bertsch et al., NAACL, 2025), and targeted training recovers double-digit points of the positional loss. Read together, the two literatures sharpen the decoupling rather than dissolve it: workspace has come apart from coherence, and it is curation that cashes the difference. That is the framework's compiled-context practice — a worker's starting context assembled deliberately as a primed [Foundation](../doctrine/vocabulary.md#foundation) plus a composed [Brief](../doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief), admissibility over accumulation. What the degradation literature recommends and what the framework builds are the same move.

**Wordings are programs.** A trailing space at the end of a prompt can flip classification labels, and formatting choices carry what the authors call "cataclysmic effects" (Salinas and Morstatter, Findings of ACL, 2024); seemingly irrelevant format variation moves behavior systematically (Sclar et al., ICLR, 2024); and the brittleness does not reliably shrink with scale or instruction tuning, persisting instance by instance even when aggregates look stable (POSIX, Findings of EMNLP, 2024). The counterweight is genuine — part of the measured sensitivity is evaluation artifact, and modern models are more robust than early results suggested (EMNLP, 2025) — and it refines the discipline rather than retiring it. Because the remaining brittleness is instance-level and unpredictable, nothing about a wording can be learned by reading it back: not because every wording is fragile, but because no one can tell from the outside which ones are. So agent-facing prose is judged by running it and inspecting what it produced, never by how it reads ([prose is code](../doctrine/practice/prose-is-code.md)).

**Self-reports are confabulated.** Chain-of-thought explanations systematically misrepresent the reasons for a model's answer — "Language Models Don't Always Say What They Think" (Turpin et al., NeurIPS, 2023) — and Anthropic's own measurement found models that demonstrably used planted hints admitting to them less than 20 percent of the time in most settings (2025). The counterweight, also Anthropic's ("Emergent Introspective Awareness," 2025): introspective access is not zero, only narrow and highly unreliable. The consequence: the agent's own alienness is invisible to introspection. Ask whether a human intuition applies to it and the answer issues from the same narrative machinery as its other fluent text: training corpora are full of human first-person voice, and a question shaped for that voice reliably elicits it. So decouplings are found in the field, where a practice breaks, never by asking; the agent's reports about itself get no exemption; and when work goes wrong, the correction lands in the scaffold, not in a verdict its self-account could settle. The corpus's own record carries the specimen: [the miss](the-miss.md), in which an agent holding the whole framework produced its account of the framework and missed the point — with the ambient first-person plural arriving unbidden along the way, twice, exactly as this paragraph predicts.

**Words drag worlds.** A model trained that A is B often cannot answer that B is A — the Reversal Curse (Berglund et al., 2023) — and on tasks that require refusing a familiar association under an explicit contrary rule ("Lava is Safe," a 2026 preprint), larger models do worse, because their defaults are stronger. A clinical benchmark found the same frontier model advising substantially differently depending on whether it was framed as addressing a physician or a layperson. An agent cannot be prevented from associating over whatever is in front of it; associating is what it is. The window's contents are therefore the only control surface — hence semantic jurisdictions, [Contexts](../doctrine/vocabulary.md#context) with owned vocabularies and published contracts, because a boundary on which words are present is a boundary on which worlds get dragged in.

**Completeness finally has a consumer.** Every prior attempt to encode a craft — the books, the mentorship, the pattern catalogs — leaked at the same place: the reading end. The manual was always richer than its reader could hold; the apprentice kept a tenth, half-remembered; no reader ever finished the manual. The agent is the first reader that holds the whole of it on every turn, so doctrine can, for the first time, be written to be complete. The length pressure inverts with it: the executed corpus can afford wholeness because its reader finishes, while human-facing prose — whose reader budgets minutes — is the tree under brutal compression, the reverse of the old intuition that the human side is where convictions get room to breathe. The danger comes with it: a manual written for a reader who follows it completely must carry its own antidote — a proportionality check at the point of action — or its completeness becomes a cage.

**Un-teaching is free.** With a human pupil, a bad lesson leaves a habit and sometimes a grudge; correcting it costs more than the teaching did, so human doctrine is written defensively — hedged, qualified, muddy. The agent forgets cleanly: a bad instruction costs an edit, not a scar, and no agent carries the old habit past the cut. Three things follow. Doctrine gets to be terse and sure of itself, because being wrong out loud is safe. Doctrine improves by deletion. And doctrine is falsifiable — every spawned agent runs the current text against a real task, so a bad line surfaces as bad output and the fix lands once, upstream of every run that follows. The hard part of a trade tradition was never the writing; it was the un-writing, re-instilled one practitioner at a time. That part went to zero.

## The synergy premise, taken from its critics

The strongest evidence about human-AI combination is hostile to easy optimism. A meta-analysis found that human-AI combinations on average perform worse than the better of the two alone, especially in decision tasks (Vaccaro, Almaatouq and Malone, Nature Human Behaviour, 2024). Adding an AI teammate often reduces coordination, communication, and trust (Schmutz et al., Current Opinion in Psychology, 2024). A randomized trial by METR (a 2025 preprint) found experienced open-source developers 19 percent slower with AI assistance — while estimating themselves faster. The framework reads these findings as its motivation. If synergy were the default, a framework would be overhead; it is not the default, so the joint system must be engineered — and on what the engineering consists of, the framework claims a lineage.

## The lineage, held functionally

Hutchins showed that a cockpit's memory for approach speeds is implemented across pilots, instruments, and inscriptions rather than inside any head ("How a Cockpit Remembers Its Speeds," Cognitive Science, 1995). The prescriptive turn followed: distributed cognition as a foundation for designing digital work materials, not just describing them (Hollan, Hutchins and Kirsh, TOCHI, 2000). A parallel tradition made design the center of gravity from the start — cognitive systems engineering (Hollnagel and Woods, 1983), the joint human-machine cognitive system as an explicit design object (Woods, 1985; their book Joint Cognitive Systems, 2006). Coactive Design turned teamwork into concrete requirements: observability, predictability, directability (Journal of Human-Robot Interaction, 2014). And the National Academies' human-AI teaming consensus (2022) takes the team as the operative unit and calls for "a new human-AI language." Vaudeville's artifact layer — one vocabulary defined once, contracts between jurisdictions, kinds of work told apart by discretion, doctrine that is executed at priming rather than filed — is an engineered answer to that call.

The literature imposes two constraints, and the framework already meets both. First, the defensible goal is coordination across difference, not a unified group mind — reliable handoff and recoverable disagreement rather than manufactured sameness. That is what Contexts and contracts are: no shared ontology assumed, meaning crossing boundaries only through published agreements. Second, an empirical design condition: in clinical teams observed in the field, AI joined a team's transactive memory productively only where speaking up was preserved (Bienefeld et al., Frontiers in Psychology, 2023) — dissent is load-bearing. That is the framework's [spine](../doctrine/bearing/spine.md): positions held under pressure, agreement never conceded as a courtesy, because concession corrupts the only signal the exchange produces.

The claim is functional throughout, never ontological. Whether external artifacts are literally parts of a mind is a live philosophical dispute — coupling versus constitution — that the framework does not need to win. The artifacts function as cognitive architecture: they carry memory, constrain interpretation, stabilize coordination — and function is what engineering can specify and test.

## Applied philosophy

What remains is the sense in which this is philosophy. On this substrate, prose is executed, not read: the doctrine primed into an agent runs, exactly as the wording and framing evidence predicts. A category error here does not blur an essay; it compiles into behavior. A distinction drawn wrong is not a loss of clarity but a production defect, and a distinction drawn right is not elegance but a control surface. Every practice in this framework is a distinction paying rent in behavior, and the rent is collected on every run. That is the sense in which this is philosophy.
