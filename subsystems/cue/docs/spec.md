# vaudeville-cue

vaudeville-cue owns what a running Bob *does* inside its own session: the volitional work it drives over its own assignment. Two siblings bound that work. vaudeville-bobiverse owns what happens *around* the session — the inter-session lifecycle of spawn, prime, closeout, and teardown — and vaudeville-hook owns the **screen**, machinery fired on the Bob from outside its volitional loop that checks the Bob and the corpus it commits. The session has two walls, then: the inter- versus intra-session wall with bobiverse, and the volition wall with the screen, which is intra-session yet outside the Bob's own loop. bobiverse brings a Bob into being and hands it cue's Brief, and from that first turn on, everything the Bob does *of its own volition* within the session is cue's.

Two intra-session concerns live here.

## The first turn

vaudeville-cue produces the first-turn body for a freshly-spawned Bob. The /spawn pipeline calls `vv assignment-context <ASSIGNMENT>` to get that body; this subrepo's job is to render it. Fetch the item from the tracker, select its first-turn template by the item's **kind**, and render it from the item's id, summary, description, accumulated comment thread, and sign-off mark. The sign-off mark sets the picker's posture toward the body: signed, the operator has seen the work and vouches that its framing represents what they meant to be discussing; unsigned, no one has, so the body is an agent's draft that may not carry the operator's intent, to press against the code rather than take on its say-so. The mark never touches the kind's own posture toward the goal: a Premise's goal stays open to press and a Direction's ends stay settled on their hinge, signed or not.

Four kinds, four first turns. A **Premise** is a claim put up to be argued, so it renders by its Route, and the Route picks the template: `check-in` surfaces a decision to settle before the work, `plan` decomposes the work into child Assignments, `explore` opens an unbounded conversation. The other three kinds each render a template chosen by kind, not by Route. A **Direction** is a settled call resting on a hinge; its turn surfaces that hinge and sends the Bob to re-derive the means from the why, reading the listed surface as a fallible sketch rather than the work. A **Command** is a directive carried out rather than pressed, clearing the *what* with the operator first when it is filed on the `check-in` route, then merging and closing through the same realization tail every Premise shares. A **Manual** hands the operator the wheel: the Bob reacts and understands but changes nothing until directed.

The templates are doctrine: each encodes what a first turn for its kind looks like. A Premise's Route templates are the spawnable Routes, and adding a Route means adding a template here. The kind templates (Direction, Command, Manual) live in their own loader, outside the Route lookup's namespace, so no stray Route value can resolve to one. A Direction always carries the `check-in` Route and a Command carries `check-in` or `direct`, but the Route lookup reads neither: a Command's Route only modulates its execute turn (the gloss it shows, and whether it stops to clear the *what*), and a Manual carries no Route at all.

Pickability gating (wrong kind, resolved/claimed Workflow, missing or unknown Route, deps-unresolved, scope) is `vv spawn-preflight`'s job upstream of this subrepo. cue assumes a valid spawnable item; an invalid one fails loudly: the Route lookup raising rather than cue absorbing it into a validation taxonomy of its own.

## The intra-session lifecycle

Once the Bob is running, the verbs that move its own work forward are cue's: **Checkpoint**, by which a Bob sheds an oversized conversation mid-run and resumes the same work in a fresh context; **Realize**, the router that is the single public front door to making a planned assignment real (it dispatches to the procedure that fits): the design-bracketed path for work carrying unsettled flow-control design (full-process for code), with agent-facing prose composition always taking that path's prose specialization, prose-process, however settled the design feels; the lighter minimal-process for a settled change that is not prose composition; or off-tree process when the deliverable never touches the git tree, and an on-tree procedure **Tenders** the implemented work to a pull request and hands it to **Parlay** to converge, while off-tree process closes through closeout instead. Prose-process holds no prose craft of its own: it partitions an assignment's prose into reader-situations and composes each through **Prose**, the composer that settles a piece's **Framing**, drafts and purges it, and tests the result by its effect with an **Assay**, before the procedure delivers all the pieces in one pull request. Alongside these run the producer-side passes a Bob takes over its own work: **Design** (which full-process runs as a matter of course), **Winnow**, and **Spike**. These ship as the skills under `scaffold/.claude/skills/`, deployed into every Bob.

Checkpoint is the central mechanism, and the only one of these verbs with backing `vv` commands. `vv digest` extracts the operator's verbatim turns from this session's transcript (the **Digest**), and `vv checkpoint` composes them with the **Carryover** the Bob authors into a **Resume Brief**, then sheds the conversation and resumes the same work in a fresh session with the Brief as its first turn. The resumed Bob wakes already grounded, never fetching its grounding from disk. The Resume Brief is the Checkpoint counterpart of the spawn-time Brief above: the same act of composing and injecting a first turn, with the Digest and Carryover where the assignment would be. The clear is irreversible, so cue holds the handoff to one invariant, a Bob is never stranded in a session it cannot leave: a checkpoint that cannot compose a faithful first turn is refused before the clear, and a clear lost after it signals the still-live session rather than failing in silence.

## Collaborators

**vaudeville-bobiverse** owns the inter-session lifecycle: the other half of the same Bob. It spawns the Bob, primes the Foundation each spawn forks from, and closes the Bob out; at spawn it invokes `vv assignment-context` to get the Brief. The Checkpoint driver is the intra-session counterpart of bobiverse's closeout-time Teardown driver: both detached processes acting on a Bob's pane, on opposite sides of the inter/intra-session boundary.

**workmux** owns the pane the Bob runs on; the Checkpoint driver drives that pane through `workmux send`. The boundary is the `workmux send` command surface.

**vaudeville-core** owns the anticorruption layer over the work tracker. Its name is a misnomer: so-called "core" is not a shared kernel but Vaudeville's outermost boundary, the layer that translates external systems (the work tracker, git hosting, the host config) into Vaudeville primitives. The assignment fetch behind `vv assignment-context` goes through "core"'s anticorruption surface; cue does not name the tracker by product.

**GitHub** (via the `gh` CLI) carries the PR operations Tender and Parlay run. The boundary is the `gh` command surface.
