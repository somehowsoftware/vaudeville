# vaudeville-cue vocabulary

vaudeville-cue's domain is what happens inside a running Bob: composing the first-turn Brief it reads at spawn, and the intra-session lifecycle it runs thereafter. Composing the Brief, the cross-context **Brief** (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief`), rendered by the item's kind (a Premise by its **Route**) from the templates [spec.md](spec.md) describes, introduces no cue-local terms of its own. The intra-session lifecycle does, and they are defined below.

Cross-context terms (**Bob**, **Assignment**, **Route**, **Brief**, **Foundation**) are linked to the framework vocabulary, not redefined. The Bob-aggregate's observable states (Spawned, Claimed, **Materialized**, Closed) belong to vaudeville-bobiverse, which owns the lifecycle *around* the session; cue's verbs run inside it and some are observable as those states, so the prose below names them without redefining them. One seam is deliberate and live: cue's [Realize](#realize) verb is observed as the **Materialized** state (the act renamed where cue owns it, the state keeping bobiverse's older name until a bobiverse assignment reconciles the pair).

## Verbs

### Realize

Turn a planned assignment into reality: implement it, then [Tender](#tender) the result for review. `/realize` is the **router**: the single public front door a Bob reaches for to realize the assignment it has been working. It carries no procedure of its own; its one job is to judge which [Procedure](#procedure) fits the assignment in front of it and to dispatch by invoking it. That judgment is the agent's reasoning structured to survive additive growth, not a decision tree, not a config lookup: the Bob at the router has been working the assignment and can see its repository, so it already holds what the choice needs. Realize is the verb the older **Materialize** named; the **Materialized** state a Bob is observed in after realizing stays bobiverse's, under its own name. The [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) belongs one level down, inside the procedure, not in the router.

### Design

The design pass a Bob runs after the goal is agreed and before implementation, settling the clean approach up front rather than discovering it through review. It consults `/panel` to sharpen the approach across five outside lenses, then commits to a design along three axes: what the domain's speech must now be able to say (the new or defective ubiquitous-language terms, each a word the domain speaks rather than a name the code needs); how it should interact with the existing code, including the refactors a clean design requires; and the domain-derived contracts the tests must keep. The committed design lives in the Bob's conversation and crosses the [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) into implementation as the [Carryover](#carryover); Design writes no durable document, so it is not a decision-record artifact. Whether it runs follows from the procedure the router selects, not from an operator's opt-in: the [full-process](#procedure) procedure runs Design as a matter of course, where a lighter procedure may omit it. It is the opening half of the [Doctrine Bracket](#doctrine-bracket). The three axes and the procedure live in the Design skill itself.

### Tender

The carry-to-PR piece a [Procedure](#procedure) ends in: once implementation has left committable changes, run local CI, commit under the assignment id, push, open the pull request, and hand off to [Parlay](#parlay). To tender is to present finished work for acceptance: the PR is the tender, and the merge that accepts it is the operator's, never the Bob's. It is factored as its own piece so every procedure that closes in a PR shares one carry-to-PR path instead of re-deriving it. Distinct from the **Contributor**, the role that tenders, and from the Bob-aggregate states the work is observed in around it, Tender is the act of carrying work to a pull request, not the actor or a state. The steps live in the Tender skill itself.

### Parlay

The convergence loop a **Materialized** Bob runs over each PR its assignment produced (usually one, several when a contract forces a coordinated change in a peer repository): address review comments, resolve conflicts against `main`, watch CI. A thin entry that [Checkpoints](#checkpoint) into `/_continue_parlay`, the convergence body.

Each round's convergence turns on the reviewer's **disposition** toward the current head — it has findings on this code, has cleared it with a sign-off, or is silent — read against the loop's **patience**, the bounded wait it gives the reviewer before silence reads as a skip rather than an unfinished review. That head-relative disposition is distinct from the whole-PR **convergence verdict** the round produces, and the two must not be conflated: a fresh sign-off only *accelerates* a PR that is already clean, mergeable, and green; patience is what guarantees a quiet PR still converges; and an open finding on the current head is what holds one back. The reviewer is treated as unreliable infrastructure throughout — it may not review, may signal late, may flake — so neither safety nor progress rests on it answering: progress is the loop's own patience, and safety is the structural absence of an unaddressed finding on the head. Exits when every PR is reviewer-clean, mergeable, and green; escalates a PR as a failure after three rounds of patching in a single run, on the premise that the volume of correct-looking patches is itself the signal of a misframing the reviewer could not catch. An escalation is the operator's to lift, never the Bob's: that lifting is a **Waiver**, the operator's judgement that the three rounds were not a fatal misframing and that the loop should resume. The round count stands through a Waiver rather than resetting, so a fresh escalation past one is a louder alarm, not a clean slate.

### Checkpoint

Shed a Bob's conversation mid-run and resume the same work in a fresh context: forward-only, nothing is rolled back to (the database sense of the word does not apply). The Bob reads the [Digest](#digest) and authors the [Carryover](#carryover); `vv checkpoint` composes the two into a [Resume Brief](#resume-brief), then reseats the session — replacing it in place (same pane, same worktree) with a fresh session born holding the Brief as its first turn; the reseat is bobiverse's primitive, the session replaced in one act, so no cleared-but-unseeded interval exists to be stranded in. A Checkpoint is how control crosses from one lifecycle skill body to the next, and every such control handoff sheds the conversation, which is why realizing an assignment through the [full-process](#procedure) procedure and then converging its PR with [Parlay](#parlay) reseats twice. The standalone primitive is `/checkpoint`; the full-process procedure and Parlay checkpoint into their continuation bodies (`/_continue_full_process`, `/_continue_parlay`), whose leading underscore marks them as machinery no operator invokes directly; a bare Checkpoint needs no continuation body: its Resume Brief closes with the work itself.

### Winnow

The per-passage prose audit a Bob runs over a PR before review: each comment and each touched documentation passage is tested against the "almost no comments" doctrine and the WHAT/HOW/why layering, then disposed of: deleted, made unnecessary by a fix to the code, relocated to the layer that owns it, or kept for the non-obvious *why* it carries. A producer-side quality pass that transitions no lifecycle state. The decision tree and its grounding live in the Winnow skill itself.

### Frame

The pass that settles a piece of agent-facing prose's approach before it is written, by consulting `/panel` from outside the author's own frame. The prose sibling of [Design](#design): where Design settles the clean approach for code along three axes, Frame settles the angle a piece takes toward its [reader-situation](#reader-situation), choosing the panel cast by where the prose would fail its reader (a rule misapplied at a borderline, or a model misread). It writes no durable document; the committed framing lives in the conversation the composition draws on. The opening bookend of [Prose](#prose), and a standalone skill in its own right. The cast choice and the pass live in the Frame skill itself.

### Assay

The effect-test a Bob runs over a finished piece of agent-facing prose: it gives fresh readers only what a real reader would hold, runs them with the prose present and with it withheld, and reads whether the prose moves what they do. Agent-facing prose is a force on the reader rather than information it absorbs, and a force is measured by its effect, not by re-reading; so the verdict is force, not correctness, across three findings: load-bearing (the prose shifts the action toward its purpose), misfiring (it shifts the action away), and inert (it changes nothing a capable reader would already do). Inert is the common verdict and the instrument's real work, since inert prose is the failure no author can see unaided. Its validity rests on one separation: the fixture-writer that composes the reader's situation is blind to the intended reading, because an author who knows what the prose should mean writes a situation that ratifies it. The closing bookend of [Prose](#prose), and a standalone skill that points at any prose, any time. It is blind by construction to the author-side defects the composition's own passes catch: a defect no reader could feel moves no reader, so it reads as inert. The method and the verdict live in the Assay skill itself.

### Prose

The composer of one coherent piece of agent-facing prose for one [reader-situation](#reader-situation): it grounds the premises it rests on against their source, settles the [Framing](#frame), drafts, purges from the reader's chair, checks every line against what the reader holds, fills the gaps, and tests the result with an [Assay](#assay), owning the loop from the assay's verdict back to the draft until the prose carries force. It stops at a finished piece and does not deliver: delivery belongs to the procedure that loops it, or to the operator when it is run by hand. `/prose` is the public composer the prose path loops over and the skill reached for to compose or sharpen a single piece by hand, an instrument like [Design](#design) and [Frame](#frame), not a router-dispatched procedure. The procedure lives in the Prose skill itself.

## Reader-situation

The reader a piece of agent-facing prose is written for, together with everything that reader holds when it reads: its [Foundation](~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#foundation), the lifecycle moment it stands at, and what it would do absent the prose. It is the unit the prose path turns on. [prose-process](#procedure) partitions an assignment's prose into reader-situations, one composed piece each; [Frame](#frame) settles a piece's approach for its reader-situation; [Prose](#prose) composes for one; and [Assay](#assay) instantiates one as the fixture it measures the prose's force against. The concept earns a name because those four uses must not drift: a piece partitioned for one reader and tested against another would never be caught, since nothing else makes the two agree. It is what the reader-position pass and its eval already circle as "where the reader stands" and "what the reader holds."

## Procedure

The session-level counterpart to [Route](~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#route). Route, set on the assignment before any session exists, classifies the shape of conversation the work expects; Procedure, chosen inside the session by the [Realize](#realize) router, classifies how the Bob realizes it. The set is open and grows additively, which is what makes the router a keystone rather than a capstone: each later procedure adds its own selection criterion and its own skill, without the router ever reaching into a procedure's internals.

The deepest cut the router makes is usually not the medium but whether the work still carries an unsettled **flow-control design**: a flow that steers a machine (code) or an agent (a skill, a doctrine passage). Work carrying one takes the **design-bracketed path**, which settles the design before producing the artifact and specializes by medium. **full-process** is its code specialization: the orderly-contribution procedure that runs [Design](#design), implements behind the [Doctrine Bracket](#doctrine-bracket), and [Tenders](#tender) the result. **prose-process** is its prose specialization, for a skill, a doctrine passage, or framing that installs a model in a capable reader. It holds no prose craft of its own: it partitions the assignment's prose into [reader-situations](#reader-situation), composes each through [Prose](#prose) (which grounds its premises against their source, settles the [Framing](#frame), drafts, purges from the reader's chair, and tests the result by its [Assay](#assay)), and [Tenders](#tender) all the pieces in one pull request. The partition is the only judgment it carries; a long run over several pieces may take a bare [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) between them, but the procedure runs none of its own. Agent-facing prose composition is the exception to that cut: it always takes prose-process, settled design or not, because for prose the design is rarely the hard part and the composition always is.

Where the design is already settled, two lighter procedures apply. **minimal-process** is selected when a change belongs in the repo and ships as a PR, carries no unsettled design (no term to work out, no domain logic, no contract to pin, no flow to settle), and is not agent-facing prose composition; it keeps the contribution tail and drops the Design pass and panel. **off-tree process** is selected when the deliverable never touches the git tree (a tracker mutation, an infrastructure or third-party state change, a process action); it takes no pull request: its review is the assignment's Route rather than a [Tender](#tender), and it hands to closeout rather than [Parlay](#parlay). full-process still reaches the same end for work whose medium was not known up front: when implementation leaves a clean working tree, its clean-tree branch refers that work to off-tree process rather than completing it, but that branch is a referring net, not a second procedure.

The router-dispatched procedures are private: each a `_`-prefixed skill no operator invokes by name, the convention the `_continue_*` continuation bodies already follow. A procedure earns a public name only when entering it is an operator-declared intent the router cannot infer: `/spike` is the one such, elected by name because the commitment to throw the work away is a choice, not something read off the artifact. The prose path's craft skills are public for a different reason: [Prose](#prose) and its [Frame](#frame) and [Assay](#assay) bookends are instruments, not procedures, the prose counterparts of [Design](#design) and `/panel`, reached standalone to compose or test a piece of prose by hand as readily as the prose procedure reaches them.

## Artifacts

### Carryover

What only the conversation built, authored by a Bob just before a [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) clears it: the synthesis, the open edge, and the next concrete action (the part the transcript does not already hold and the [Digest](#digest) cannot derive). The resumed Bob reads the Carryover to continue mid-stride rather than re-plan. Authored, not derived; its counterpart across the reseat is the Digest.

### Digest

The operator's verbatim turns, extracted mechanically from the session transcript and line-located back into it, so a [Checkpoint](#checkpoint)'s resumed Bob rebuilds operator intent from primary source rather than a summary. Recovers turns the operator relayed indirectly through a `tmp` file as well as typed ones, and is cumulative across the Bob's checkpoints: a later Checkpoint keeps every earlier session's turns. `vv digest` prints it into the Bob's context. Derived, never authored; its counterpart across the reseat is the [Carryover](#carryover), which is authored, never derived.

### Resume Brief

The first user turn a freshly-reseated Bob reads: a grounding frame, the [Digest](#digest), the [Carryover](#carryover), and a closing instruction naming the continuation body to invoke, or, for a bare [Checkpoint](#checkpoint), the work to continue. Composed by `vv checkpoint` from the Digest and Carryover. The Checkpoint counterpart of the spawn-time Brief (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief`): the same act (a first turn composed and injected, never fetched) with the Digest and Carryover where the assignment would be. One Resume Brief per Checkpoint.

### Doctrine Bracket

The bracketing discipline that frames implementation between two reads of the design Doctrine within the [full-process](#procedure) procedure. The opening half is the now-mandatory [Design](#design) pass: run warm, before the [Checkpoint](#checkpoint), it maps the assignment's intended domain logic onto the existing UL and commits to the terms, structure, and contracts the work needs. The closing half runs cold, after implementation: re-read the Doctrine with the diff in hand and revise before [Tendering](#tender). The two halves sit on opposite sides of the checkpoint (language-formation in the warm context that has the conversation, the diff-read in the fresh context that implements), and the bracket's specific questions live in the procedure's skills.
