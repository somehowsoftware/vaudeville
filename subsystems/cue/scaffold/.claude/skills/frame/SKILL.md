# Frame

Before a word is written, settle how to come at it. The framing is the approach a piece of prose takes toward its reader: what the reader must end up able to do, where it would go wrong, and the angle that leaves it most resilient in the situations you did not foresee. Settling the framing first is the prose analogue of [`/design`](../design/SKILL.md): the clean approach decided up front, rather than discovered through review after the prose is composed.

The framing is settled for a particular reader-situation: a particular reader holding particular things at a particular moment. The same idea framed for a freshly-spawned Bob and for an operator scanning many sessions wants different approaches; name the reader before you choose the angle.

## When to use

- As the opening bookend of [`/prose`](../prose/SKILL.md): the composer settles the framing here before it drafts.
- Standalone, when an approach is worth getting right before any prose exists: a hard rule whose borderline you cannot yet see how to state, a model you are unsure how to install.

## When not to use

- **Mechanical editing.** A rename, a link fix, prose whose approach was already settled elsewhere: there is no framing to find, and forcing the pass is the invention the design doctrine warns against.

## The pass

1. **Frame the question as constraints and opportunities, not a solution to ratify.** A panel handed a narrow read or a leading question echoes your preconceptions back. Bring it the reader, the situation, and what the prose must achieve, framed as "an agent in this scaffold must do X given Y; what approach leaves it most resilient across the futures it might meet?", not as "how do I word this to get X?"
2. **Choose the cast by where the prose would fail its reader.** Run `rule-stress` when the prose states a rule a reader must apply at a borderline; run `expository` when it installs a model the reader must hold. Most prose does both, so choose by the danger that would hurt most: the rule misapplied, or the model misread. Run both when one pass would leave the other danger live.
3. **Consult [`/panel`](../panel/SKILL.md).** Invoke it via the Skill tool, naming the cast. The panel is Socratic: it sharpens your thinking, it does not hand you the answer. You hold what it does not: this work, the reader, what the prose is in service of.
4. **Commit to the framing.** Read the panel through your own context and decide on the approach. A framing is a position, not a survey; where the panel split, say what the split turned on and which way you came down. If the pass surfaces a new or defective domain term, grow the vocabulary here, before the prose depends on it.

## Non-goals

- **Writes no durable document.** The committed framing lives in this conversation and is consumed by the composition that follows; a persistent approach-note is the decision-record anti-pattern the intent doctrine rules out.
- **Does not compose.** It settles the how and stops. The drafting is the composer's, downstream of the framing this pass commits to.
- **Does not re-implement `/panel`.** It calls the shipped skill; tuning a cast is a pull request against `/panel`, not a bespoke consult here.
