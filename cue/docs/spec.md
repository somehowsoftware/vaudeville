# vaudeville-cue

vaudeville-cue owns what a running Bob *does* inside its own session: the volitional work it drives over its own assignment. Two siblings bound that work. vaudeville-bobiverse owns what happens *around* the session — the inter-session lifecycle of spawn, prime, closeout, and teardown — and vaudeville-hook owns the **screen**, machinery fired on the Bob from outside its volitional loop that checks the Bob and the corpus it commits. The session has two walls, then: the inter- versus intra-session wall with bobiverse, and the volition wall with the screen, which is intra-session yet outside the Bob's own loop. bobiverse brings a Bob into being and hands it cue's Brief, and from that first turn on, everything the Bob does *of its own volition* within the session is cue's.

Two intra-session concerns live here.

## The first turn

vaudeville-cue produces the first-turn body for a freshly-spawned Bob. bobiverse's spawn invokes its configured downstream — `vv assignment-context <ASSIGNMENT>`, this subrepo's verb — to get that body; this subrepo's job is to render it. Fetch the item from the tracker, select its first-turn template by the item's **kind**, and render it from the item's id, summary, description, accumulated comment thread, and sign-off mark. The sign-off mark sets the picker's posture toward the body: signed, the operator has seen the work and vouches that its framing represents what they meant to be discussing; unsigned, no one has, so the body is an agent's draft that may not carry the operator's intent, to press against the code rather than take on its say-so. The mark never touches the kind's own posture toward the goal: a Premise's goal stays open to press and a Direction's ends stay settled on their hinge, signed or not.

Four kinds, four first turns. A **Premise** is a claim put up to be argued, so it renders by its Route, and the Route picks the template: `check-in` surfaces a decision to settle before the work, `plan` decomposes the work into child Assignments, `explore` opens an unbounded conversation. The other three kinds each render a template chosen by kind, not by Route. A **Direction** is a settled call resting on a hinge; its turn surfaces that hinge and sends the Bob to re-derive the means from the why, reading the listed surface as a fallible sketch rather than the work. A **Command** is a directive carried out rather than pressed; filed on the `check-in` route, its turn clears the *what* with the operator before the work. A **Manual** hands the operator the wheel: the Bob reacts and understands but changes nothing until directed.

The templates whose Bob will carry work to done — check-in, direction, execute — render a shared **realization brief**: the boundary facts a Bob needs at the moment its goal settles. It renders only where that moment is coming: a plan Bob delivers Assignments, an explore Bob follows a conversation, a Manual Bob's intent arrives live.

The templates are doctrine: each encodes what a first turn for its kind looks like. A Premise's Route templates are the spawnable Routes, and adding a Route means adding a template here. The kind templates (Direction, Command, Manual) live in their own loader, outside the Route lookup's namespace, so no stray Route value can resolve to one. A Direction always carries the `check-in` Route and a Command carries `check-in` or `direct`, but the Route lookup reads neither: a Command's Route only modulates its execute turn (the gloss it shows, and whether it stops to clear the *what*), and a Manual carries no Route at all.

Pickability gating (wrong kind, already claimed or resolved, missing or unknown Route, deps-unresolved, scope) is `vv spawn-preflight`'s job upstream of this subrepo. cue assumes a valid spawnable item; an invalid one fails loudly: the Route lookup raising rather than cue absorbing it into a validation taxonomy of its own.

## The intra-session lifecycle

Once the Bob is running, cue equips it two ways.

The skills, deployed into every Bob under `scaffold/.claude/skills/`, are the working kit the Bob reaches into as its own judgment calls for: **Tender** carries finished work to a pull request and watches its CI; **Design** settles the clean approach for code where the design is still unsettled; **Prose** composes agent-facing prose piece by piece, with **Frame** and **Assay** as its bookends, both usable alone, and **routing-force-assay** as Assay's specialization for standing indexes; **Winnow** audits the comments and documentation a PR touches; **Spike** works throwaway code for learning under a prior commitment that it never ships; **Panel** puts a question to a cast of outside lenses. Each skill holds its own procedure and is reached when its work is in front of the Bob.

**Checkpoint** is the lifecycle mechanism, and the only one of these verbs with backing `vv` commands. `vv digest` extracts the operator's verbatim turns from this session's transcript (the **Digest**), and `vv checkpoint` composes them with the **Carryover** the Bob authors into a **Resume Brief**, then sheds the conversation and resumes the same work in a fresh session with the Brief as its first turn. It resumes by **reseating** the Bob: a detached process replaces the pane's session in place — same pane, same worktree, same assignment, the *seat* conserved — with a fresh session born holding the Resume Brief, so the resumed Bob wakes already grounded, never fetching its grounding from disk. Reseating is bobiverse's primitive (`vv reseat`), the intra-session sibling of spawn: cue composes the Brief, names the worktree whose pane to reseat, and reads the model the session is running on so the reseat carries it rather than reverting to a launch default — an operator naming a model on `/checkpoint` overrides the read one. The rest of the launch knowledge that births a Bob (remote-control, autonomy) stays bobiverse's, in one place, along with the default model bobiverse chooses when cue reads none. The Resume Brief is the Checkpoint counterpart of the spawn-time Brief above: the same act of composing and injecting a first turn, with the Digest and Carryover where the assignment would be. The reseat is the handoff's only destructive act and it is irreversible, so cue holds the handoff to one invariant, a Bob is never stranded in a session it cannot leave: a checkpoint that cannot compose a faithful first turn is refused before the reseat, leaving the live session untouched.

The chain also bounds its own depth. A session shed while its Digest holds no operator turn and no commit landed since the session before it is a **stall**: an irreversible, lossy compression that advanced nothing under no supervision. When the consecutive-stall run reaches its bound, `vv checkpoint` composes the Resume Brief's closing to **surface and wait** — the resumed Bob opens by reporting where the work stands and holds for the operator rather than resuming mid-stride — so a chain compressing itself unwatched and unproductive surfaces its own runaway instead of drifting past recovery. The two guardrails divide cleanly: the screen governs shed cadence within a session, this governs chain depth across them. It is never a refusal of the checkpoint, which would strand the full context the invariant above exists to protect.

## Collaborators

**vaudeville-bobiverse** owns the inter-session lifecycle: the other half of the same Bob. It spawns the Bob, primes the Foundation each spawn forks from, and closes the Bob out; at spawn it invokes its configured downstream (`vv assignment-context`, above) for the Brief. It also owns **reseat** (`vv reseat`), the born-grounded session replacement Checkpoint hands its handoff to — the intra-session sibling of spawn and of bobiverse's closeout-time Teardown, all acting on a Bob's pane through the launch knowledge bobiverse keeps in one place. Checkpoint composes the Resume Brief, names the worktree, and resolves the model the reseated session runs on; reseating it is bobiverse's. The boundary is the `vv reseat <worktree> <brief-path> [--model <model>]` command surface, a detached launch composed in `vv checkpoint`.

**vaudeville-core** owns the anticorruption layer over the work tracker. Its name is a misnomer: so-called "core" is not a shared kernel but Vaudeville's outermost boundary, the layer that translates external systems (the work tracker, git hosting, the host config) into Vaudeville primitives. The assignment fetch behind `vv assignment-context` goes through "core"'s anticorruption surface, and the Component register cue reads is core's host-config view; cue does not name the tracker by product.

**GitHub** (via the `gh` CLI) carries the PR operations Tender runs. The boundary is the `gh` command surface.
