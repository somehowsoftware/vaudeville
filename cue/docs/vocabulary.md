# vaudeville-cue vocabulary

vaudeville-cue's domain is what happens inside a running Bob: the first-turn Brief it reads at spawn, and the work it drives inside its session thereafter. The Brief side introduces no cue-local terms: it renders the cross-context **Brief** (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief`) from the templates [spec.md](spec.md) describes, selected by the Assignment's kind (for a Premise, by its **Route**). The intra-session side has a vocabulary of its own, defined below.

## Checkpoint

Shed a Bob's conversation mid-run and resume the same work in a fresh context. A working conversation grows until it no longer fits the window that must hold it; what the work needs forward is not the whole transcript but the operator's actual words and the synthesis only the conversation built. So the Bob reads the [Digest](#digest) and authors the [Carryover](#carryover); `vv checkpoint` composes the two into a [Resume Brief](#resume-brief), then reseats the session — replacing it in place (same pane, same worktree) with a fresh session born holding the Brief as its first turn. Forward-only: nothing is rolled back to; the database sense of the word does not apply. The reseat is bobiverse's primitive, the session replaced in one act; and because it is the handoff's one destructive, irreversible act, the whole mechanism holds to a single invariant — a Bob is never stranded in a session it cannot leave: a checkpoint that cannot compose a faithful first turn is refused before the reseat, leaving the live session untouched. A Checkpoint costs the work its warm context, so it is spent, not scheduled: taken at a seam, under genuine pressure.

### Digest

The operator's verbatim turns, extracted mechanically from the session transcript and line-located back into it, so a [Checkpoint](#checkpoint)'s resumed Bob rebuilds operator intent from primary source rather than a summary. Recovers turns the operator relayed indirectly through a `tmp` file as well as typed ones, and is cumulative across the Bob's checkpoints: a later Checkpoint keeps every earlier session's turns. Alongside the turns it recovers the session's [unconfirmed queued messages](#unconfirmed-queued-message), quarantined under a warning and never mixed into the turn stream, so operator content the harness lost is surfaced without being mistaken for authority. `vv digest` prints it into the Bob's context. Derived, never authored; its counterpart across the reseat is the [Carryover](#carryover), which is authored, never derived.

### Carryover

What only the conversation built, authored by a Bob just before a [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) clears it: the synthesis, the open edge, and the next concrete action. The [Digest](#digest) can always be re-derived from the transcript; the Carryover is the one thing nothing can recover — it lives in the conversation being shed, so it is authored now or lost. The resumed Bob reads it to continue mid-stride rather than re-plan. Authored, not derived; its counterpart across the reseat is the Digest.

### Unconfirmed queued message

Operator content recovered from a session's queue-operation trail that the harness never persisted as a delivered turn. A message the operator queues while the assistant is busy and then edits in the composer before it is delivered reaches the model's live context and is acted on, yet is written to the transcript only as its raw queue operations, never as a user turn — its text survives nowhere else. The [Digest](#digest) recovers that text so a lost instruction is not silently dropped across a [Checkpoint](#checkpoint), but it is categorically **not** one of the Digest's operator turns: whether a given one was a live instruction the operator expected carried out or a draft queued and then retracted is undecidable from the trail, since the two share a single fate — enqueued, then removed, with no persisted turn. So the Digest surfaces it as unconfirmed rather than as authority, and the resumed Bob, the only reader that can weigh it against the work actually done, is the one that adjudicates it. It earns a name apart from the turn because it is the one thing the Digest carries that is not verbatim authority: a construct that wears intent's shape but cannot be confirmed as intent must be marked off from the turn, never folded into it.

### Resume Brief

The first user turn a freshly-reseated Bob reads: a grounding frame, the [Digest](#digest), the [Carryover](#carryover), and the work to continue. Composed by `vv checkpoint` from the Digest and Carryover. The Checkpoint counterpart of the spawn-time Brief (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief`): the same act (a first turn composed and injected, never fetched) with the Digest and Carryover where the assignment would be. One Resume Brief per Checkpoint.

### Stall

A [Checkpoint](#checkpoint) that sheds while making neither operator contact — no turn in its session's [Digest](#digest) — nor forward progress — no commit landed since the session before it. A single stall is already the failure it names: an irreversible, lossy compression that advanced nothing with nobody watching, whose one benign-looking cousin (a Bob delivering heads-down) is exactly the one a landed commit tells apart, so a second stall only deepens the drift. When the consecutive-stall run reaches its bound, the [Resume Brief](#resume-brief)'s closing turns from resume-mid-stride to **surface and wait**: the resumed Bob reports where the work stands and holds for the operator, so the chain surfaces its own **runaway** rather than compressing on, unwatched, past recovery. The commit is a deliberately lossy proxy for progress — a session doing real but uncommitted work, a solo investigation or an off-tree deliverable, reads as stalled — a cost taken knowingly, since a false stall is one surfaced status the operator waves through while a missed one is the drift the bound exists to catch. Distinct from the reseat invariant: that refuses a checkpoint which cannot compose a faithful Brief, whereas a stall never refuses the checkpoint, since refusing would strand the very context the invariant protects.

## Design

The pass that settles the clean approach for code after the goal is agreed and before implementation, so the approach is decided up front rather than discovered through review. It consults `/panel` to sharpen the approach from outside the author's own frame, then commits to a design along three axes: what the domain's speech must now be able to say (the new or defective ubiquitous-language terms, each a word the domain speaks rather than a name the code needs); how the work should interact with the existing code, including the refactors a clean design requires; and the domain-derived contracts the tests must keep. Run when the work carries a design still unsettled — a term to work out, a contract to pin, a flow to shape — and skipped when it does not, a judgment made fresh per piece of work, not per assignment. The committed design lives in the conversation that made it; Design writes no durable document. The three axes and the pass live in the Design skill itself.

## Reader-situation

The reader a piece of agent-facing prose is written for, together with everything that reader holds when it reads: its [Foundation](~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#foundation), the lifecycle moment it stands at, and what it would do absent the prose. It is the unit prose composition turns on: an assignment's prose is partitioned into reader-situations, one composed piece each, and the composer picks that granularity; [Frame](#frame) settles a piece's approach for its reader-situation; [Prose](#prose) composes for one; [Assay](#assay) instantiates one as the fixture it measures the prose's force against. The concept earns a name because those uses must not drift: a piece partitioned for one reader and tested against another would never be caught, since nothing else makes the two agree. The same concept anchors Prose's line-by-line check — every line resolved against where the reader stands and what it holds — and the reader-position eval that calibrates that check.

## Prose

The composer of one coherent piece of agent-facing prose for one [reader-situation](#reader-situation): it grounds the premises it rests on against their source, settles the [Framing](#frame), drafts, purges from the reader's chair, checks every line against what the reader holds, fills the gaps, and tests the result with an [Assay](#assay), owning the loop from the assay's verdict back to the draft until the prose carries force. Agent-facing prose is code run by its reader, which is why composing it takes a discipline this deliberate rather than an editor's read-through. When an assignment's prose spans several reader-situations, the partition is the composer's first judgment: cut by reader-situation, compose each piece through this loop. Prose stops at a finished piece; it does not deliver. The procedure lives in the Prose skill itself.

## Frame

The pass that settles a piece of agent-facing prose's approach before it is written, by consulting `/panel` from outside the author's own frame. The prose sibling of [Design](#design): where Design settles the clean approach for code along three axes, Frame settles the angle a piece takes toward its [reader-situation](#reader-situation), choosing the panel cast by where the prose would fail its reader (a rule misapplied at a borderline, or a model misread). It writes no durable document; the committed framing lives in the conversation the composition draws on. The opening bookend of [Prose](#prose), and a standalone skill in its own right. The cast choice and the pass live in the Frame skill itself.

## Assay

The effect-test a Bob runs over a finished piece of agent-facing prose: it gives fresh readers only what a real reader would hold, runs them with the prose present and with it withheld, and reads whether the prose moves what they do. Agent-facing prose is a force on the reader rather than information it absorbs, and a force is measured by its effect, not by re-reading; so the verdict is force, not correctness, across three findings: load-bearing (the prose shifts the action toward its purpose), misfiring (it shifts the action away), and inert (it changes nothing a capable reader would already do). Inert is the common verdict and the instrument's real work, since inert prose is the failure no author can see unaided. Its validity rests on the fixtures: the fixture-writer is blind to the intended reading, because an author who knows what the prose should mean writes a situation that ratifies it; and the situations include those where the prose's claims come under strain, not only the ones it was written around. The closing bookend of [Prose](#prose), and a standalone skill that points at any prose, any time. It is blind by construction to the author-side defects the composition's own passes catch: a defect no reader could feel moves no reader, so it reads as inert. The method and the verdict live in the Assay skill itself.

## Winnow

The per-passage prose audit a Bob runs over a PR before review: each comment and each touched documentation passage is tested against the "almost no comments" doctrine and the WHAT/HOW/why layering, then disposed of: deleted, made unnecessary by a fix to the code, relocated to the layer that owns it, or kept for the non-obvious *why* it carries. A producer-side quality pass over the Bob's own work. The decision tree and its grounding live in the Winnow skill itself.

## Tender

Carry finished work to a pull request and present it for acceptance. To tender is to present: acceptance belongs to the PR gate, and the one flat rule — owned by `~/.vaudeville/doctrine/code/architecture.md` — is that the merge into the line the work targets, main or the Track branch feeding it, is never the Bob's own act. The gate exists to put a second judgment between the author and an irreversible commit. Everything else about a tender's shape is the work's to determine: usually one PR to main, though neither the count nor the target is Tender's to fix — a contract can force coordinated PRs in peer repositories, and a Track member's PR targets its Track's branch. Once the PR is open, Tender watches its CI and reports; the watch is bounded, and where the repository defines no CI, it reports that instead of waiting on a signal that cannot come. Presenting is not completing: whether a merged PR is the assignment's goal or a station on the way to it is the goal's own question, and Tender does not answer it. The steps and the watch live in the Tender skill itself.
