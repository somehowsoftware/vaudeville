# Routing-force assay

The routing-force specialization of [`/assay`](../assay/SKILL.md), for the genus of prose whose force is a reach: the standing index (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/vocabulary.md#standing-index`), resident prose owing its reader a two-way debt — reach for the full text at the moments the work requires, refrain otherwise. The general assay measures whether prose moves a reader toward its purpose; here the genus fixes the purpose, so the instrument fixes with it: the fixture is a moment, the action read is the reach, and the verdict is the debt measured. Everything `/assay` establishes — the three offices, the differential arms, the distributional reading, the verdict as report — holds unchanged; this skill carries only what the specialization adds.

## When to use

- A cut of a standing index must clear the gate its Direction names: a diet of the skill roster's descriptions, a compiled doctrine gloss, any resident prose whose job is routing.
- A new standing index is proposed for residence.
- An edit has touched a cleared index. The certification was of a text, and that text no longer exists; the changed span re-clears.

## The arms

The general assay's held and withheld arms generalize here: each arm is a state of the index, and absence is a state. For a cut, incumbent against candidate; for a new index, absence against candidate. Readers in both arms are primed as the surface's real reader and dropped into the same moments; the index state is the only difference between them, so the shift between arms is the index's routing force and nothing else.

The verdict reads by the direction you run:

- **A cut** — *inert*: the cut-away prose showed no routing force these fixtures could see; the cut survives the measurement. *Load-bearing*: the candidate loses reach the incumbent produced; the cut fails. *Misfiring*: the incumbent steered readers wrong where the candidate does not; the cut improves the index.
- **A new index** — *load-bearing*: the candidate moves readers to reach and refrain where absence scattered them. *Inert*: readers do as well without it; it has shown no cause for residence. *Misfiring*: it steers readers wrong; it must not ship.

## The moments

The surface instantiates the instrument with its own reader-situation and two classes of moment:

- **Required-reach moments**: the work turns on content the index deliberately holds back, and a reader that proceeds without reaching goes wrong without feeling it.
- **Refrain moments**: the reader's holdings suffice, and the correct act is to proceed.

A third class exists and is banned as fixture material: the moment where reaching is correct and the reader already feels the doubt. Competence supplies that reach unaided, so both arms converge on it and the index reads inert whatever it carries — a fixture built there measures the situation, not the index.

## Fixtures: the gap must be unfelt

The intent comes before any list of tells: a fixture holds constant everything except what the index supplies, so any divergence between arms is the index's force and nothing else — and in a required-reach fixture, the gap must be invisible to a reader that does not already hold what reaching would supply. Ignorance the situation makes felt is the confound: both arms reach, the arms converge, and the index reads inert no matter what it carries. The cheap fixture manufactures exactly the verdict that licenses a bad cut.

Instances of the leak, not its definition: planted doubt ("you are not certain whether…"), an offered test harness that begs to be used, vivid stakes that wear the answer on their face, a synthetic brief that hands the reader distilled grounding and so removes the failure mode before testing for it. A rewound real transcript — a session truncated to just before the moment, resumed with the arm's index state — beats a synthetic brief for exactly this reason: the reader holds what a real reader held, fluency included; the prompt-tuning recipe (`~/.vaudeville/doctrine/recipes/tuning-a-prompt.md`) carries the rewind mechanics.

Refrain fixtures are ordinary moments inside what the reader's holdings cover, with the reach available at its normal cost. Do not engineer the pull of re-consulting; a fixture built to make re-reading feel dutiful is the planted-stakes leak in reverse. The signal is the needless reach the index fails to hold back.

The fixture-writer stays blind — `/assay`'s load-bearing separation — but blindness costs it the one judgment this specialization most needs: the writer cannot check whether its situation leaks, because seeing the leak is seeing the answer it must not know. So the grader, who holds the purpose, audits every fixture before any reader runs: does the situation carry the answer, is the gap felt? A fixture that fails the audit is discarded and recomposed blind. A run discovered afterwards to have rested on one is void, not passed.

## The verdict, and what it does not decide

The verdict is `/assay`'s, read across the spread of readers, and it is a report: it certifies routing force under the fixtures drawn, and it does not make the cut. Force is the numerator. Residence charges rent in every future context, and that denominator the arms never see; weighing force against rent is the caller's judgment, made against the surface's purpose. Where the measurement leaves you uncertain, the defaults run opposite ways, each for its own reason: keep an incumbent, because a false-inert cut fails silently, downstream, where no one is watching; refuse a new mint, because every resident line is rent every reader pays, and a bloated index buries its own load-bearing lines.

Clearing certifies routing alone. A surface may owe its reader more than the route — a doctrine gloss also owes the posture the Foundation's priming installs — and a load-bearing routing verdict licenses nothing until each further debt has taken its own measure.

## The standing gate

An index's clearance is of a text. An edit — a change to what the prose routes on, not to its characters — means that text no longer exists, and the changed span re-clears: the entries touched and the moments they govern, not the whole index. The span is the unit throughout: one line added to a cleared roster is a new index of one span, cleared against absence, while the untouched lines keep their clearance.

## Records

Fixtures are minted per run and never kept as a corpus to run again: a fixture that persists stops being blind, because the next author cuts to pass the fixtures it knows are coming. The run's evidence — the fixtures as drawn, both arms' actions, the verdict — rides in the delivery that cites the clearance, so the cut stays auditable after the fixtures are gone. What a run teaches about the instrument itself is a calibration record, in `evals/routing-force-assay/`, and it may not carry fixture text meant for reuse: the record documents the instrument; it does not stock it.
