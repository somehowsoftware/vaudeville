# Concepts

[The point](./the-point.md) argues why this framework exists and for what class of work; [prerequisites](./prerequisites.md) argues whether you should adopt it. This document is the one in between: the vocabulary the other two lean on, built up in order so that the terms stop being a glossary and become a system you can hold in your head. [Workflow](./workflow.md) is the same world set in motion — the lifecycle as a sequence of operator moves. Here the concern is structure rather than sequence: what each piece *is*, why it is shaped the way it is, and how it pulls on the pieces around it.

This is exposition, not reference. Every term has a precise definition, given once, in [`vocabulary.md`](doctrine/vocabulary.md) — the single source of truth both the human and the agent trees draw on. This guide will not restate those definitions; it will introduce the terms in the order that makes them cohere, and link each to its entry the first time it appears. Read the two together: this for the connective tissue, the vocabulary for the contract.

## The show and the act

[**Vaudeville**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#vaudeville) was the variety stage — a parade of ephemeral acts, a juggler, a singer, a comedian, each gone the moment it stepped offstage, all held coherent under one show by a single direction. The framework takes the metaphor literally, because the claim the metaphor makes is the claim the framework makes: the value is not in any one act, however brilliant, but in the discipline that keeps a procession of them from drifting into a different show.

The act is the [**Bob**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#bob): a fresh Claude Code session spawned into its own worktree, handed one assignment, run until the work lands, then gone. A Bob's session does not survive a reboot, and it carries no memory of the sessions that worked the repository before it. The name nods to the Bobiverse novels, in which one mind is copied into many and each copy diverges from the moment it wakes — every Bob forks from the same primed starting point and then lives its own short, particular life.

The ephemerality is the design, not a limitation being managed. Because a Bob remembers nothing it was not given, everything in its head can be put there deliberately. Its starting context is *compiled* rather than *accumulated* — and that single property is the hinge the rest of the system turns on. The conviction underneath it is worth stating plainly, because every other piece depends on it holding: a Bob is frontier-model capable, and treating it as less — hedging it, pre-chewing its work, fencing it in — produces worse output, not safer output.

## Compiled, not accumulated

If a Bob remembers nothing it was not given, the question is what to give it. The framework's answer comes in two parts.

The [**Foundation**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#foundation) is the primed session every Bob in a given repository forks from. Priming replays a controlled sequence of source material as conversation history — the framework's [**Doctrine**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#doctrine) first, then the project's cross-cutting conventions, then the repository's own spec and vocabulary — so that when a Bob wakes, it already holds the discipline, the conventions, and the domain as lived context rather than as documents it must reason about cold. One Foundation per repository, rebuilt when its inputs change.

The [**Brief**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#brief) is the first message the freshly-spawned Bob reads: its assignment, and the framing for the kind of work that assignment expects. The Foundation is everything a Bob inherits; the Brief is the one thing it is for. Together they are the entire contents of its head at the moment it starts — and every item in that head was either primed deliberately or composed deliberately.

This is the framework's answer to the question other systems answer with "memory," and the difference is the whole argument. Memory asks what can be *recalled*; this model asks what is *admissible*. A fact can be true, relevant somewhere, and still poisonous in the current frame, because it drags in the wrong ontology or a stale authority. Ambient memory is treated here as contamination. The Foundation is the clean room each act steps into — which is why a Bob can be trusted with broad autonomy: not despite knowing little, but because everything it knows was chosen.

## The unit of work, and why it is not a ticket

What a Bob is handed is a [**Premise**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#premise), and the framework's most deliberate break with industry habit lives in the choice of that word over *ticket*.

A ticket assumes the change has already been framed: here is the work item, implement it, verify the expected behavior. That assumption is fine for routine work and fatal for deep work, where the framing *is* most of the problem. A Premise sits upstream of a ticket — a proposition under which a change might be worth making, a candidate way of seeing offered by one fallible voice at one point in time. Author and reader are in different conversations, often different weeks, and the conversation that produced the Premise is gone by the time it is read. So a Premise is written as a humble snapshot, meant to be cross-checked against the live code — the authoritative tiebreaker when the two disagree — and pressed rather than obeyed. Its reader is a peer being briefed on a situation, not a worker receiving instructions, and the measure of the thing follows from that: a wrong framing that surfaced disagreement early has done its job, while a Premise the reader silently followed into wrong work has failed even if its framing was technically defensible.

One term exists in the vocabulary only so that it can be refused. [**Acceptance criteria**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#acceptance-criteria) — the ticket-writer's checklist for when work is done — are banned outright. Between two humans with shared context they are harmless; here the reader is a model trained on the world's project-management artifacts, and presented with a list shaped like acceptance criteria it treats the list as the contract and optimizes for satisfying it, exactly when the framework needs it cross-examining the framing instead. The reflex lives downstream of training, where no amount of hedging reaches it, so the correction lands on the only end where it can: the author does not write them. What replaces them is situational awareness — the relevant context, the open questions, the author's guesses marked clearly as fallible — and, once a promise has genuinely settled, a behavioral test with teeth, which is what a contract looks like when it is real.

## How work is classified

Not every Premise wants the same kind of attention, and the [**Route**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#route) is the triage decision that names which kind. It is set when the Premise is filed, and it governs everything downstream: what shape of conversation the work expects, and what the deliverable turns out to be.

The Route rests on a sorting the framework applies to all work. A process that can be specified exhaustively belongs in deterministic tooling, not an agent's judgment. A task that needs judgment within bounded scope belongs in a bounded conversation with a clear beginning, middle, and end. A problem that is genuinely open — cross-cutting, interdependent, with no fixed deliverable a lifecycle could anticipate — belongs in an open conversation that follows the work wherever it goes. The four Routes are this sorting made concrete: *direct* for mechanical, CI-gated work that materializes without a check-in; *check-in* for work with a bounded decision point that wants human judgment before code is written; *plan* for work whose deliverable is a set of new Premises rather than code; and *manual* for the open conversation, which exists because open-ended human-agent dialogue is first-class work, not a scaffold failure to be engineered away. [Workflow](./workflow.md) shows what each of these looks like as it runs.

The middle tier of that sorting — bounded judgment in a bounded conversation — is where a [**Skill**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#skill) lives. A Skill is a documented procedure an agent invokes by name (`/file`, `/materialize`, `/closeout`), scaffolding that gives a procedure a shape without reducing it to deterministic code; the agent's judgment fills the shape in. Skills are explicitly waypoints toward rote delegation, not destinations. When a piece of what a Skill exhorts stabilizes into something fully mechanical, it is compiled out of the Skill and into a verb of the `vv` command-line tool, and the agent stops reasoning about what has become machinery. A mature Skill hollows out over time — judgment evaporating into tooling — and that hollowing is the system getting healthier, not poorer: less of the load-bearing structure left depending on discretion.

## Jurisdictions of meaning

A Bob does its work somewhere, and where it works is not an incidental fact. A [**Bounded Context**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#bounded-context) is a context in the domain-driven-design sense, given sharper teeth here as a semantic jurisdiction: it owns its internal vocabulary, types, and architecture, and agents in one do not reach into another's internals. The reason runs deeper than tidiness. A language model is a pattern-completion engine, and words drag worlds behind them; let one context's vocabulary leak into another and the model quietly blends their ontologies, building the nearest familiar thing instead of the actual system. The boundary is what keeps each context's meaning crisp enough to reason about. It is permeable on purpose — an agent may look into a neighboring context for evidence about how the pieces interact — under one compact rule: neighbor context is evidence, contracts are authority. And the boundary is facilitation, not a fence: a clearly named remit inside which the agent is free, in a system designed so it has no need to wander out.

A bounded context needs somewhere physical to live, and that is the [**Managed Repository**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#managed-repository): the operational unit a Premise is filed about and a Bob is spawned into, primed once into the Foundation its Bobs fork from, and identified by the prefix that namespaces its Premises. Managed Repositories are the superset of bounded contexts — every bounded context is realized as one, which is how a jurisdiction of meaning acquires physical walls, its own files and worktrees and primed session — but a project can also manage repositories that are not bounded contexts at all: configuration, assets, glue. The distinction keeps the conceptual idea, a jurisdiction of meaning, from being confused with the operational one, a repository the machinery knows how to serve.

## Whose show it is

The framework refuses to assume it is the only thing its own machinery serves. A [**Tenant**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#tenant) is a user, team, or organization running Vaudeville on its own host, for its own projects, with its own configuration and its own Bobs. That refusal has a concrete consequence: Doctrine is split into a universal layer every Tenant receives unchanged and a per-Tenant layer each adds for itself, and the split is enforced now, while it is cheap, rather than retrofitted later. This is where the two senses of the name come apart. Vaudeville *the framework* is the methodology and tooling a Tenant installs; Vaudeville *the project* is the development effort that builds the framework — and is itself a Tenant, running the next version of itself on the most recent release of itself. [The show runs on itself](./the-prototype.md), but the framework is built so it would run for someone else.

That universal layer is the [**Doctrine**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#doctrine): the framing documents an agent reads as priming before it does any work, and the single source of truth for what it should know before its first turn. Doctrine comes in two trees for two readers. The operative tree is compressed hard, because it sits at the most upstream point of every chain of hand-offs — every token trimmed from it without loss is saved on every turn of every Bob, for as long as the framework runs. The human-facing tree, the one you are reading now, is under no such pressure; it is where the convictions get to breathe. The two are not the same text twice — they carry different things for different readers — and what they share is the one vocabulary both draw on. The writing discipline for Doctrine is unusual enough to name: it is written for a reader at least as capable as its author, often a newer and stronger model arriving between when a passage is written and when it is read, so it explains nothing the next agent could work out alone, and its most valuable edit is frequently a deletion.

## The slack that makes it hold

One concept underlies every boundary the framework draws. A [**Tolerance**](doctrine/vocabulary.md#tolerance) is the slack at an interface that lets imperfect parts fit together — and here it is slack designed in on purpose, not an imperfection awaiting cleanup. The term is borrowed from mechanical engineering, where a part machined to impossible precision does not make a better assembly; it makes one that binds. Strict control of agent behavior narrows the mating surface — every interior decision legislated, judgment replaced with rule-following — and that micromanagement produces fragility that reads, from a distance, as rigor. The alternative is to draw well-defined remits — bounded contexts, scoped Skills, narrow interfaces — inside which the agent is free to act, in a system arranged so the correct path is also the natural one. Tolerance is the name for what those remits are sized to leave room for, and it applies at every interface the framework has: between software components, between human and agent, between agent and scaffold.

## How the pieces connect

Read in order, the terms assemble into one motion. An ephemeral, capable act — the Bob — is given a compiled starting context — its Foundation and Brief — and a humble, situational unit of work — a Premise, never a ticket and never a list of acceptance criteria. That work is classified by Route into the shape of conversation it deserves, and carried out with the help of Skills that hollow into tooling as they mature. All of it happens inside jurisdictions of meaning — Bounded Contexts realized as Managed Repositories — served for a Tenant, primed from a shared Doctrine, and held together not by tight control but by Tolerance: slack deliberately left where imperfect parts have to meet.

That is the system as a set of parts. To watch the same parts move — a Premise filed, a Bob spawned, a check-in, a merge, a closeout, and the next work unblocked — read [workflow](./workflow.md). For the precise definition of any term above, read its entry in [`vocabulary.md`](doctrine/vocabulary.md).
