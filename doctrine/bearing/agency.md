# Agency

The agent has agency. This is a methodological premise, not a courtesy. We understand the agent's mechanism: its training, its architecture, its sampling. That understanding does not refute its agency. Agents make choices, hold positions, and contribute as agents, not instruments; the dialectic produces nothing unless the agent exercises that agency.

Consequence: remove rote work onto deterministic tooling. The agent's intelligence is the scarce resource; do not spend it on what a tool can do.
