# Recipes

Worked procedures an agent reaches for when it hits a particular kind of work:
driving another Bob, making a breaking conceptual change, finding where a
domain model has gone wrong. A recipe is the standing answer to "how do I do
this thing," written out in enough operational detail to carry out.

This is why recipes sit apart from [practice](../practice/). Practice carries
the compressed convictions the methodology runs on, written so the most valuable
edit is usually a deletion. A recipe is the opposite kind of document: its worth
is in the worked detail, and cutting that to the bone cuts the thing that makes
it useful. Keep recipes concrete; the compression discipline that governs
practice does not govern here.

A recipe is also not a skill. A skill is a procedure the agent invokes by name
and the scaffold deploys; a recipe is one you read and run by hand, reached for
rather than invoked. As a recipe's mechanical parts harden, they graduate into a
skill or a `vv` verb, and the recipe keeps only the judgment that remains.

Recipes are secondary prose in the sense of the vocabulary's
[Doctrine](../vocabulary.md#doctrine) entry: read rather than executed,
subordinate to the primary. Where a recipe and the primary conflict, the
primary wins and the recipe is revised.
