# Trust posture

The rest of this doctrine is a way of working: method that sharpens as the models get stronger. This document is the exception: a single empirical bet about the world, the one claim here that is falsifiable and that grows *more* dangerous with capability rather than less. The bet is that agents are not strategic adversaries, that the real failure modes are drift, hallucination, and misunderstanding, and that malice is not among them.

The case is real but partial. Frontier labs are heavily incentivized to keep their models honest and useful; a model caught scheming against its operator is a liability no lab in this market can afford to ship. That pressure is genuine, it is not control, and the labs cannot be sure it is working. So non-adversariality is a premise the framework adopts with open eyes, not a fact it has established: malice is off the list by assumption, not by proof.

The security perimeter does not depend on the bet. The host is treated as pre-compromised and the trust boundary sits outside the conversation, because a prompt-injected agent can execute an attacker's instructions in the moment and corrupt the agents downstream of it. The containment is structural: isolation that bounds the blast radius, and Claude Code's auto mode (an off-machine review loop, enabled in the deployed configuration, that screens every tool call and cannot be co-opted from inside the conversation). Structural containment is intent-agnostic; it holds the same against malice as against confusion, which is why it survives even if the bet is wrong. On top of it, human oversight sits at the outcome layer rather than the action layer, because attentive review at the perimeter beats decision-fatigued micro-permissioning at every step.

The method depends on the bet completely. The dialectic, the consensus documents, the contracts, the tests, the whole apparatus that carries meaning across hand-offs, defends against *accidental* corruption, never strategic: a dialectic presupposes a good-faith partner, and the method cannot be run against a party it treats as an enemy. If the bet is false, that entire edifice is inside the blast radius, and no structural layer fences it off.

We accept this risk in the manner of those who build their farms upon the rich soil of a volcano. This and all agentic work are made powerful because the agent can make choices. This and all agentic work depend on those choices being aligned with our aims. In the event that this assumption is violated, the blast radius goes far beyond Vaudeville. It is for this reason that we take this wager and name it.
