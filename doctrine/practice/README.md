# Practice

The methodology of the work — coordinating capable, ephemeral AI agents on an interdependent system. Distinct from project management (assumes human continuity), software engineering (assumes humans write the code), and ML engineering (assumes the model is the artefact under construction).

## Dialectical engineering

Vaudeville is one instantiation of *dialectical engineering*. Central observation: all parties are unreliable. The real-world mechanism for catching what an unreliable party has missed is opposable conversation with a second unreliable party whose failure modes are different. The work product is what the dialectic surfaces; the methodology is the discipline that keeps the dialectic productive.

Scope: software that exceeds the grasp of a single human intelligence, where the *control problem* — keeping a coherent picture of a system under constant change, and keeping every contributor's actions aligned with that picture — is the dominant term in the cost of building it.

Four primary tools:

- **Domain-driven design** — the discipline's primary substrate is shared vocabulary between human and AI.
- **Dialectic** — the only way to surface the failure modes of unreliable parties is opposable conversation.
- **Humor** — the working environment has to make unconfident truth-telling cheaper than confident wrong-telling.
- **Decomposition** — the boundaries between subsystems are themselves a work product, not a side effect.
