# Signal-side leverage under noisy measurement

The rate-limiting failure mode in Vaudeville is information corruption across lossy transmissions: specs into Assignments, Assignments into prep, prep into a [Bob](../vocabulary.md#bob)'s work, work into code. Measurement of where corruption concentrates is itself noisy: failures are randomly distributed and discrete.

Under noisy measurement, fixes at the producer end of a chain (better specs, better Assignments, less ambiguous skills) compound across every downstream read. Fixes at the consumer end (better prep, better review) help once, at the read site. Prefer signal-side fixes; they multiply.

Prefer cheap, legible producer-side fixes over expensive ones: measurement noise means a big bet on one intervention can fail to move the system measurably even when correctly aimed, while a cheap fix gives a real result and a cleaner reading of the next bottleneck.

This is orthogonal to storage medium (agent memory systems, code-review pipelines). The bottleneck is the lossiness of the chain of custody at each transmission boundary, not memory capacity or code quality.
# Place assignment boundaries to maximize coherence
Every boundary loses signal, so chosen boundaries deserve deliberate placement. An Assignment boundary leaks (whatever the upstream understood that does not cross the edge is gone) while it contains, sparing the downstream agent the rest. Place Assignment boundaries where the dependency graph is cheap to traverse: a narrow, named contract crossing each edge, not reasoning the reader must reconstruct.

A boundary has a time as well as a place. The contract crossing an edge must exist before the work depending on it is cut, or the edge names a contract its downstream agent has to invent, so lay meaning before its implications: settle the names and contracts first, then cut the work that is an implication of them. And because finishing that work reshapes what should follow it, decompose in rounds rather than all at once: cut what is an implication of meaning now settled, and defer the rest to a later cut made against the world the early rounds produced, not the forecast standing in for it up front.

# Publish the meaning-move before anything builds against it

A meaning-move — a breaking conceptual change: a term re-carved, a genus renamed, a contract re-drawn — cannot be scheduled. It arises mid-work, when a seam breaks an assumption, so no rule of the form "one conceptual change in flight at a time" is keepable. The keepable invariant is a publication gate: the move must be present in main before anything outside the work that carries it builds against it. Main is where meaning is published; downstream work gates on publication. A break discovered mid-work therefore halts nothing and entitles nothing: carry the move in the work that found it, land it, and only then does it exist to build against.

The gate is safe on this substrate because a divergence held whole in one context window is coherent — the agent attends to the old world and the new at once — while the same divergence spread across windows that do not share it is poison: an agent reasons from the names in front of it. A [Track](../vocabulary.md#track) — a jurisdiction in time — bounds the divergence to the work that holds it, and main stays honest for everyone else. Two moves colliding on the same term reconcile at merge order: the later integrator reconciles against the meaning already landed. The worked procedure for landing a move — language first, then the world renamed under green tests, then behavior — is [language-before-code](../recipes/language-before-code.md).

# Composition rigor scales with the reader's correction loop

The same compounding governs how hard a piece of agent-facing prose, the prose one agent writes for another to act on, must be worked before it ships. A flaw in prose the reader absorbs as authority is paid on every downstream read; a flaw the reader is positioned to catch is paid once or never. Impact sets the floor and profile never lowers it. *This is small, single-use, settled* is the reflex that ships corrupted prose, not a reason to work it less. What earns a lighter authoring pass is one thing only: a reader with a live correction loop. A Premise reader is told to treat the body as a fallible snapshot, check it against the live code as the authoritative tiebreaker, and surface disagreement before acting, so the author may trust that backstop for what it catches (a wrong claim, a wrong mechanism guess) and owns at full strength only the residue a pressing reader will not flag because it does not read as wrong against the code: project-manager register, smuggled acceptance criteria, dictated mechanism, dogwhistle ordering. That lighter pass is not a lower standard but the complementary one.

The relief is void for the whole body the instant any part of the prose installs a model the reader carries forward without re-deriving. A Premise that fixes framing, vocabulary, or how a domain works is durable prose in a single-use costume, and earns the full process though it is read once. A reader who executes rather than contests has no correction loop at all. A Command body is single-use like a Premise, but its reader carries out the steps instead of pressing them, so its author owns every flaw class as if writing doctrine. Reuse profile tracks the correction loop until it does not, and the cases where they part are exactly the ones that ship corrupted prose: follow the loop, not the shadow.
